Probability

Back to Probability and Statistics Topics

Probability is a measure of the likelihood that an event will occur. It quantifies uncertainty and is used extensively in various fields such as statistics, mathematics, finance, science, and engineering.

Basic Probability

The probability of an event \( A \), denoted by \( P(A) \), is defined as:

\[ P(A) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of possible outcomes}} \]

Example

Consider a fair six-sided die. The probability of rolling a 3 is:

\[ P(\text{rolling a 3}) = \frac{1}{6} \]

Probability Axioms

  • Non-negativity: For any event \( A \), \( P(A) \geq 0 \).
  • Normalization: The probability of the sample space \( S \) is 1, i.e., \( P(S) = 1 \).
  • Additivity: For any two mutually exclusive events \( A \) and \( B \), \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) \).

Conditional Probability

The conditional probability of an event \( A \) given that event \( B \) has occurred, denoted by \( P(A \mid B) \), is defined as:

\[ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \]

provided \( P(B) > 0 \).

Example

Consider a deck of 52 cards. The probability of drawing an Ace given that the card drawn is a spade is:

\[ P(\text{Ace} \mid \text{Spade}) = \frac{P(\text{Ace} \cap \text{Spade})}{P(\text{Spade})} = \frac{\frac{1}{52}}{\frac{13}{52}} = \frac{1}{13} \]

Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' Theorem relates the conditional and marginal probabilities of random events. It is given by:

\[ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} \]

Example

Consider a medical test for a disease. Let \( D \) be the event that a person has the disease and \( T \) be the event that the test is positive. Bayes' Theorem can be used to find the probability that a person has the disease given that the test is positive:

\[ P(D \mid T) = \frac{P(T \mid D) \cdot P(D)}{P(T)} \]

Law of Total Probability

The law of total probability states that if \( \{B_i\} \) is a partition of the sample space \( S \), then for any event \( A \),

\[ P(A) = \sum_{i} P(A \mid B_i) \cdot P(B_i) \]

Example

Consider a factory that produces widgets. The widgets can come from one of three machines. Let \( A \) be the event that a widget is defective, and let \( B_i \) be the event that the widget comes from machine \( i \). The law of total probability can be used to find the overall probability that a widget is defective:

\[ P(A) = P(A \mid B_1) \cdot P(B_1) + P(A \mid B_2) \cdot P(B_2) + P(A \mid B_3) \cdot P(B_3) \]

Applications

  • Risk Assessment: Probability is used to assess risks in finance, insurance, and engineering.
  • Decision Making: Probability helps in making informed decisions under uncertainty.
  • Statistics: Probability forms the foundation of statistical inference and hypothesis testing.
  • Games and Gambling: Probability is used to analyze and strategize in games of chance.

Conclusion

Probability is a fundamental concept in mathematics and statistics that provides a framework for quantifying uncertainty. Understanding probability is essential for analyzing random events and making informed decisions in various fields.
