Hypothesis Tests

Back to Probability and Statistics Topics

Hypothesis testing is a statistical method used to make decisions about a population parameter based on sample data. It involves formulating a null hypothesis and an alternative hypothesis, then using statistical evidence to determine whether to reject the null hypothesis.

Steps in Hypothesis Testing

  1. Formulate Hypotheses: Define the null hypothesis (\( H_0 \)) and the alternative hypothesis (\( H_1 \)).
  2. Choose Significance Level: Select a significance level (\( \alpha \)), commonly set at 0.05 or 0.01.
  3. Collect Data: Gather sample data and calculate the test statistic.
  4. Determine Critical Region: Identify the critical region based on the chosen significance level and the distribution of the test statistic.
  5. Make Decision: Compare the test statistic to the critical region to decide whether to reject the null hypothesis.

Types of Hypothesis Tests

  • z-test: Used to test the mean of a population when the population variance is known and the sample size is large (\( n > 30 \)).
  • t-test: Used to test the mean of a population when the population variance is unknown and the sample size is small (\( n \leq 30 \)).
  • chi-squared test: Used to test the independence of two categorical variables or the goodness of fit of an observed distribution to an expected distribution.

Example: One-Sample z-test

Suppose we want to test whether the average height of a population is 170 cm. We collect a sample of 50 individuals with a mean height of 172 cm and a known population standard deviation of 5 cm.

Step 1: Formulate Hypotheses

\[ \begin{align*} H_0 &: \mu = 170 \\ H_1 &: \mu \neq 170 \end{align*} \]

Step 2: Choose Significance Level

Let \( \alpha = 0.05 \).

Step 3: Collect Data

Sample mean (\( \bar{x} \)) = 172 cm, population standard deviation (\( \sigma \)) = 5 cm, sample size (\( n \)) = 50.

Step 4: Determine Critical Region

Calculate the test statistic:

\[ z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu_0}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}} = \frac{172 - 170}{5 / \sqrt{50}} \approx 2.83 \]

Step 5: Make Decision

For \( \alpha = 0.05 \), the critical values are \( \pm 1.96 \). Since 2.83 > 1.96, we reject the null hypothesis.

Applications

  • Medical Research: Hypothesis testing is used to determine the effectiveness of new treatments or drugs.
  • Quality Control: Hypothesis testing is used to assess whether products meet certain standards or specifications.
  • Market Research: Hypothesis testing is used to make decisions based on sample data about consumer preferences and behaviors.

Conclusion

Hypothesis testing is a fundamental tool in statistics that allows researchers to make data-driven decisions and draw conclusions about a population based on sample data. Understanding the steps and types of hypothesis tests is crucial for conducting and interpreting statistical analyses.
