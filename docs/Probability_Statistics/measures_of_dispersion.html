Measures of Dispersion

Back to Probability and Statistics Topics

Measures of dispersion describe the spread or variability of a dataset. The most common measures of dispersion are range, variance, and standard deviation.

1. Range

The range is the difference between the highest and lowest values in a dataset. It is calculated as:

\[ \text{Range} = \text{Maximum value} - \text{Minimum value} \]

2. Variance

Variance measures the average squared deviation of each value from the mean. The formula for variance (\( \sigma^2 \)) is:

\[ \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} (x_i - \mu)^2 \]

where \( N \) is the number of values, \( x_i \) are the values, and \( \mu \) is the mean of the values.

3. Standard Deviation

Standard deviation is the square root of the variance and provides a measure of the average distance of each value from the mean. It is calculated as:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} (x_i - \mu)^2} \]

Example

Consider the dataset: \[ 3, 5, 2, 9, 5, 7 \]

  • Mean: \[ \mu = \frac{3 + 5 + 2 + 9 + 5 + 7}{6} = \frac{31}{6} \approx 5.17 \]
  • Variance: \[ \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{6} \left( (3 - 5.17)^2 + (5 - 5.17)^2 + (2 - 5.17)^2 + (9 - 5.17)^2 + (5 - 5.17)^2 + (7 - 5.17)^2 \right) \approx 6.97 \]
  • Standard Deviation: \[ \sigma = \sqrt{6.97} \approx 2.64 \]

Applications

  • Range: The range is used to understand the spread of data at a glance and identify outliers.
  • Variance: Variance is used to measure the degree of spread in a dataset, often in finance and scientific research.
  • Standard Deviation: Standard deviation is widely used to measure variability in fields such as finance, quality control, and scientific research.

Conclusion

Understanding measures of dispersion is essential for assessing the variability in data. Each measure provides different insights into the spread of the data, making them valuable tools in statistics.
