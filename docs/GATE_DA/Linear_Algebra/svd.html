Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) is a factorization method for matrices that generalizes the eigendecomposition of a square matrix to any \( m \times n \) matrix. SVD is widely used in signal processing, statistics, and machine learning.

Definition

For any \( m \times n \) matrix \( A \), the SVD is given by:

\[ A = U \Sigma V^T \]

where:

  • \( U \) is an \( m \times m \) orthogonal matrix.
  • \( \Sigma \) is an \( m \times n \) diagonal matrix with non-negative real numbers on the diagonal, known as the singular values.
  • \( V \) is an \( n \times n \) orthogonal matrix, and \( V^T \) is its transpose.

Properties

  • The columns of \( U \) are the left singular vectors of \( A \).
  • The columns of \( V \) are the right singular vectors of \( A \).
  • The singular values in \( \Sigma \) are the square roots of the eigenvalues of \( A^T A \) and \( A A^T \).

Example

Consider the matrix:

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

The SVD of \( A \) is:

\[ U = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \Sigma = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad V = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

So:

\[ A = U \Sigma V^T = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

Applications

SVD has numerous applications, including:

  • Data Compression: SVD is used in techniques like Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for reducing the dimensionality of data while retaining the most important features.
  • Signal Processing: SVD is used to filter noise from signals and images.
  • Recommender Systems: SVD is used in collaborative filtering methods for making recommendations.
  • Image Compression: SVD is used to compress images by keeping only the largest singular values and corresponding vectors.

Conclusion

Singular Value Decomposition is a powerful and versatile tool in linear algebra with a wide range of applications in data science, signal processing, and machine learning. Understanding SVD and its properties is essential for leveraging its capabilities in various fields.
