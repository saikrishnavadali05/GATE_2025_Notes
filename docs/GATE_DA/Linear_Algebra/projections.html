Projections

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

In linear algebra, a projection is a linear transformation from a vector space to itself such that applying the transformation twice is the same as applying it once. Projections are used in various applications, including computer graphics, statistics, and numerical analysis.

Definition

A projection matrix \( P \) is a square matrix that satisfies the property:

\[ P^2 = P \]

This means that applying the projection matrix \( P \) to a vector more than once does not change the result.

Orthogonal Projection

An orthogonal projection projects a vector onto a subspace such that the error (difference between the vector and its projection) is orthogonal to the subspace. The projection matrix \( P \) that projects onto the column space of a matrix \( A \) is given by:

\[ P = A(A^T A)^{-1} A^T \]

Example

Consider a vector \( v \) and a subspace spanned by a vector \( u \). The projection of \( v \) onto \( u \) is given by:

\[ \text{proj}_u v = \frac{u \cdot v}{u \cdot u} u \]

If \( u = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( v = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \), then:

\[ \text{proj}_u v = \frac{1 \cdot 3 + 2 \cdot 4}{1^2 + 2^2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} = \frac{11}{5} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \frac{11}{5} \\ \frac{22}{5} \end{pmatrix} \]

Properties of Projection Matrices

  • Idempotent: \( P^2 = P \)
  • Symmetric: For orthogonal projections, \( P = P^T \)

Applications

Projections are used in various applications, such as:

  • Least Squares Regression: In statistics, projection matrices are used to project observed data onto the space of the linear model.
  • Computer Graphics: Projection matrices are used to project 3D points onto a 2D screen.
  • Numerical Analysis: Projections are used in iterative methods for solving linear systems.

Conclusion

Projections are powerful tools in linear algebra with wide-ranging applications. Understanding the properties and computation of projection matrices is essential for various fields of science and engineering.
