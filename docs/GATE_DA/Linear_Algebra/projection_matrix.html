Projection Matrix

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

A projection matrix is a square matrix that gives a vector projection from one space to another. Projection matrices are widely used in various fields, including computer graphics, statistics, and machine learning.

Definition

A projection matrix \( P \) is a matrix such that when it is applied to a vector \( x \), it projects \( x \) onto a subspace \( V \). The projection matrix has the property:

\[ P^2 = P \]

This property indicates that applying the projection matrix twice is the same as applying it once.

Orthogonal Projection

Orthogonal projection involves projecting a vector onto a subspace such that the projection error (the difference between the vector and its projection) is orthogonal to the subspace. The projection matrix \( P \) that projects onto the column space of a matrix \( A \) (where \( A \) has linearly independent columns) is given by:

\[ P = A(A^T A)^{-1} A^T \]

Example

Consider the matrix \( A \) and vector \( x \):

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad x = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \]

The projection matrix \( P \) is computed as:

\[ P = A(A^T A)^{-1} A^T \]

First, calculate \( A^T A \):

\[ A^T A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

Then, calculate \( (A^T A)^{-1} \):

\[ (A^T A)^{-1} = \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

Now, calculate \( A^T \):

\[ A^T = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

Finally, compute the projection matrix \( P \):

\[ P = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

The result is:

\[ P = \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 & 2 \\ -1 & 2 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

The projection of \( x \) onto the column space of \( A \) is then:

\[ Px = \frac{1}{3} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 & 2 \\ -1 & 2 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

Properties of Projection Matrices

  • Idempotent: \( P^2 = P \)
  • Symmetric: \( P = P^T \)

Applications

Projection matrices are used in various applications, such as:

  • Least Squares Regression: In statistics, projection matrices are used to project the observed data onto the space of the linear model.
  • Computer Graphics: Projection matrices are used to project 3D points onto a 2D screen.

Conclusion

Projection matrices are powerful tools in linear algebra with wide-ranging applications. Understanding their properties and how to compute them is essential for many areas of science and engineering.
