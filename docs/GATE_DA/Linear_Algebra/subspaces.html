Subspaces

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

In linear algebra, a subspace is a vector space that is wholly contained within another vector space. Subspaces are important in understanding the structure of vector spaces and their properties.

Definition

A subset \( W \) of a vector space \( V \) is called a subspace if \( W \) is itself a vector space under the operations of vector addition and scalar multiplication defined on \( V \). Formally, \( W \) is a subspace of \( V \) if:

  • The zero vector of \( V \) is in \( W \).
  • For every \( u, v \in W \), the sum \( u + v \in W \).
  • For every \( u \in W \) and every scalar \( c \), the scalar product \( cu \in W \).

Examples

Consider the vector space \( \mathbb{R}^3 \). Some examples of subspaces of \( \mathbb{R}^3 \) include:

  • The set containing only the zero vector: \( \{(0, 0, 0)\} \).
  • A line through the origin, such as \( \{(x, 0, 0) \mid x \in \mathbb{R} \} \).
  • A plane through the origin, such as \( \{(x, y, 0) \mid x, y \in \mathbb{R} \} \).

Properties

Subspaces have several important properties:

  • Closed under addition: If \( u \) and \( v \) are in a subspace \( W \), then \( u + v \) is also in \( W \).
  • Closed under scalar multiplication: If \( u \) is in a subspace \( W \) and \( c \) is a scalar, then \( cu \) is also in \( W \).
  • Contains the zero vector: The zero vector of the larger vector space \( V \) is also in the subspace \( W \).

Basis and Dimension

The basis of a subspace is a set of linearly independent vectors that span the subspace. The number of vectors in the basis of a subspace \( W \) is called the dimension of \( W \), denoted as \( \dim(W) \).

Example

Consider the vector space \( \mathbb{R}^2 \) and the subspace \( W = \{(x, 2x) \mid x \in \mathbb{R} \} \). The set \( \{(1, 2)\} \) is a basis for \( W \) because it is linearly independent and spans \( W \). The dimension of \( W \) is 1.

Applications

Subspaces are used in various applications, including:

  • Solving linear systems: Understanding the solution space of a system of linear equations.
  • Fourier analysis: Decomposing functions into orthogonal components.
  • Principal component analysis (PCA): Reducing the dimensionality of data while preserving variance.

Conclusion

Subspaces are a fundamental concept in linear algebra that provide insight into the structure and properties of vector spaces. They are crucial for various applications in mathematics, physics, engineering, and computer science.
