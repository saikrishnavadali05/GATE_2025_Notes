Frequency Ranges in 5G NR

5G NR operates in two main frequency ranges defined by 3GPP:

  • FR1: 410 MHz to 7.125 GHz (Sub-6 GHz)
  • FR2: 24.25 GHz to 52.6 GHz (mmWave)

Frequency Range 1 is suitable for wider coverage and better penetration through buildings. These are typically reused 4G LTE bands and new 5G-specific mid-bands.

  • Examples: n78 (3.5 GHz), n41 (2.6 GHz), n28 (700 MHz)

Frequency Range 2 is known as millimeter wave (mmWave) and supports ultra-high bandwidth for applications like AR/VR and massive video streaming. However, it has limited range and poor penetration.

  • Examples: n257 (26 GHz), n258 (28 GHz), n260 (39 GHz)

Spectrum allocation varies by country based on regulatory authorities. For example:

  • India: 700 MHz (n28), 3.3–3.67 GHz (n78), 26 GHz (n258)
  • USA: 600 MHz (n71), 2.5 GHz (n41), 28 GHz (n261)
  • Europe: 3.4–3.8 GHz (n78), 26 GHz (n258)

5G supports carrier aggregation across frequency ranges to boost throughput. For example, combining FR1 and FR2 allows both coverage and speed.
