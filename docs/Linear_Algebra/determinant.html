Determinant

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

The determinant is a scalar value that can be computed from a square matrix and encapsulates some of its important properties. Determinants are widely used in linear algebra, calculus, and differential equations.

Definition

For a square matrix \( A \), the determinant is denoted as \( \det(A) \) or \( |A| \). The determinant of a \( 2 \times 2 \) matrix \( A \) is defined as:

\[ \det\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} = ad - bc \]

For a \( 3 \times 3 \) matrix \( A \), the determinant is given by:

\[ \det\begin{pmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{pmatrix} = a(ei - fh) - b(di - fg) + c(dh - eg) \]

Properties of Determinants

  • Multiplicative Property: The determinant of the product of two matrices is the product of their determinants: \( \det(AB) = \det(A) \det(B) \).
  • Determinant of the Identity Matrix: The determinant of the identity matrix is 1.
  • Row Operations: The determinant changes in predictable ways under row operations:
    • Swapping two rows multiplies the determinant by -1.
    • Multiplying a row by a scalar multiplies the determinant by the same scalar.
    • Adding a multiple of one row to another row does not change the determinant.
  • Transpose: The determinant of a matrix is equal to the determinant of its transpose: \( \det(A^T) = \det(A) \).
  • Determinant of Triangular Matrices: The determinant of a triangular matrix (upper or lower) is the product of its diagonal elements.

Example Calculations

Consider the matrix \( A \):

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 \\ 5 & 6 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

The determinant of \( A \) is calculated as:

\[ \det(A) = 1 \cdot (1 \cdot 0 - 4 \cdot 6) - 2 \cdot (0 \cdot 0 - 4 \cdot 5) + 3 \cdot (0 \cdot 6 - 1 \cdot 5) \]

\[ = 1 \cdot (0 - 24) - 2 \cdot (0 - 20) + 3 \cdot (0 - 5) \]

\[ = -24 + 40 - 15 \]

\[ = 1 \]

Applications

Determinants are used in various applications, such as:

  • Solving Linear Systems: Determinants are used in Cramer's rule to solve systems of linear equations.
  • Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors: The characteristic equation, which is used to find the eigenvalues of a matrix, involves the determinant.
  • Area and Volume: Determinants are used to compute the area of parallelograms and the volume of parallelepipeds in vector calculus.

Conclusion

The determinant is a powerful tool in linear algebra that captures important properties of matrices and has wide-ranging applications in mathematics, physics, and engineering.
