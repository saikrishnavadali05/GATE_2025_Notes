Idempotent Matrix

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

An idempotent matrix is a matrix which, when multiplied by itself, yields the same matrix. Idempotent matrices are significant in various fields, including statistics and linear algebra.

Definition

A square matrix \( A \) is said to be idempotent if:

\[ A^2 = A \]

This means that multiplying the matrix by itself does not change the matrix.

Properties

  • Eigenvalues: The eigenvalues of an idempotent matrix are either 0 or 1.
  • Trace: The trace of an idempotent matrix (the sum of the diagonal elements) is equal to the rank of the matrix.
  • Diagonalizability: Idempotent matrices are diagonalizable, meaning they can be expressed in the form \( PDP^{-1} \), where \( D \) is a diagonal matrix and \( P \) is an invertible matrix.

Examples

Consider the matrix:

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

This matrix is idempotent because:

\[ A^2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} = A \]

Another example is the projection matrix \( P \) which projects vectors onto a subspace spanned by a vector \( u \):

\[ P = \frac{uu^T}{u^T u} \]

For instance, if \( u = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \), then:

\[ P = \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

This matrix is idempotent because:

\[ P^2 = \left( \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \right) \left( \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \right) = \frac{1}{4} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 \end{pmatrix} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} = P \]

Applications

Idempotent matrices have various applications, including:

  • Statistics: In regression analysis, the hat matrix (or projection matrix) used to project observed values onto fitted values is idempotent.
  • Linear Algebra: Idempotent matrices are used to study projections and invariant subspaces.

Conclusion

Idempotent matrices are an important class of matrices with unique properties and significant applications in mathematics and statistics. Understanding these matrices helps in analyzing projections and linear transformations.
