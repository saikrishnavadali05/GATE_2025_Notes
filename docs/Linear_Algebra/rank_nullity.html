Rank-Nullity Theorem

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

The Rank-Nullity Theorem is a fundamental result in linear algebra that relates the dimensions of the kernel and the image of a linear transformation to the dimension of the domain of the transformation. It has significant implications in solving linear systems and understanding the structure of linear maps.

Definitions

Consider a linear transformation \( T: V \to W \) where \( V \) and \( W \) are vector spaces. The rank of \( T \), denoted \( \text{rank}(T) \), is the dimension of the image of \( T \). The nullity of \( T \), denoted \( \text{nullity}(T) \), is the dimension of the kernel of \( T \).

Rank-Nullity Theorem

The Rank-Nullity Theorem states that for a linear transformation \( T: V \to W \):

\[ \text{dim}(V) = \text{rank}(T) + \text{nullity}(T) \]

where \( \text{dim}(V) \) is the dimension of the domain \( V \).

Example

Consider the matrix \( A \) representing a linear transformation \( T: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3 \):

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

To find the rank and nullity of \( T \), we perform row reduction on \( A \) to obtain its row echelon form:

\[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \]

The rank of \( A \) is the number of non-zero rows, which is 2. Therefore, \( \text{rank}(A) = 2 \).

The dimension of the domain \( \mathbb{R}^3 \) is 3. Using the Rank-Nullity Theorem, we have:

\[ 3 = \text{rank}(A) + \text{nullity}(A) \]

Thus, \( \text{nullity}(A) = 3 - 2 = 1 \).

Implications

The Rank-Nullity Theorem has several important implications:

  • Solving Linear Systems: The rank of the coefficient matrix gives the number of linearly independent equations, while the nullity gives the number of free variables in the solution.
  • Dimension Theorem: The theorem provides a relationship between the dimensions of the domain, image, and kernel of a linear map, which is crucial for understanding linear transformations.
  • Matrix Rank: The rank of a matrix is a fundamental concept in linear algebra, and the Rank-Nullity Theorem provides a way to compute it and understand its properties.

Conclusion

The Rank-Nullity Theorem is a cornerstone of linear algebra that connects the dimensions of different subspaces associated with a linear transformation. It is essential for solving linear systems and understanding the structure of linear maps and matrices.
