LU Decomposition

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

LU decomposition is a matrix factorization method where a matrix is decomposed into the product of a lower triangular matrix and an upper triangular matrix. This technique is widely used to solve systems of linear equations, compute determinants, and find matrix inverses.

Definition

For a given square matrix \( A \), LU decomposition finds two matrices \( L \) and \( U \) such that:

\[ A = LU \]

where \( L \) is a lower triangular matrix and \( U \) is an upper triangular matrix.

Steps to Compute LU Decomposition

The process of finding the matrices \( L \) and \( U \) involves the following steps:

  1. Set \( L \) to be the identity matrix and \( U \) to be the matrix \( A \).
  2. Perform Gaussian elimination on \( U \) to make it an upper triangular matrix, while applying the same row operations to \( L \) to maintain the equality \( A = LU \).

Example

Consider the matrix \( A \):

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 6 & 3 \end{pmatrix} \]

Step-by-Step LU Decomposition

Start with \( L = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( U = A \).

Perform Gaussian elimination to make \( U \) an upper triangular matrix:

  1. Eliminate the entry below the pivot (4 in the first column):

    2. Multiply the first row by \(\frac{6}{4} = 1.5\) and subtract from the second row:

    \[ U = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 6 - 1.5 \cdot 4 & 3 - 1.5 \cdot 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 0 & -1.5 \end{pmatrix} \]

  2. Update \( L \) to keep the equality \( A = LU \):

    3. Set \( L_{21} = 1.5 \):

    \[ L = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1.5 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \]

Now, \( L \) and \( U \) are:

\[ L = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1.5 & 1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad U = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 0 & -1.5 \end{pmatrix} \]

Applications

LU decomposition is used in various applications, including:

  • Solving Linear Systems: LU decomposition allows for efficient solutions of linear systems \( Ax = b \) by solving \( Ly = b \) and then \( Ux = y \).
  • Computing Determinants: The determinant of \( A \) can be easily computed as the product of the diagonal elements of \( U \) (or \( L \)).
  • Finding Inverses: LU decomposition can be used to find the inverse of a matrix by solving multiple linear systems.

Conclusion

LU decomposition is a fundamental matrix factorization method with wide-ranging applications in numerical analysis and linear algebra. Understanding how to compute and apply LU decomposition is essential for solving complex mathematical problems.
