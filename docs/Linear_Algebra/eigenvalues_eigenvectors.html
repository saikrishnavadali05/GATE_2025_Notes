Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Back to Linear Algebra Topics

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors are fundamental concepts in linear algebra, with applications across many fields including physics, engineering, and computer science.

Definition

Given a square matrix \( A \), an eigenvector \( v \) and its corresponding eigenvalue \( \lambda \) satisfy the equation:

\( A v = \lambda v \)

where \( v \) is a non-zero vector and \( \lambda \) is a scalar.

Finding Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

To find the eigenvalues of a matrix \( A \), solve the characteristic equation:

\( \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \)

where \( I \) is the identity matrix of the same dimension as \( A \).

Once the eigenvalues are found, the corresponding eigenvectors can be found by solving the equation:

\( (A - \lambda I)v = 0 \)

Example

Consider the matrix:

 A = [4  1]
        [2  3]

To find the eigenvalues, solve the characteristic equation:

\( \det \begin{pmatrix} 4-\lambda & 1 \\ 2 & 3-\lambda \end{pmatrix} = 0 \)

This simplifies to:

\( (4-\lambda)(3-\lambda) - 2 = \lambda^2 - 7\lambda + 10 = 0 \)

The solutions to this quadratic equation are:

\( \lambda_1 = 5, \lambda_2 = 2 \)

To find the eigenvectors, solve:

\( (A - 5I)v = 0 \)

and

\( (A - 2I)v = 0 \)

For \( \lambda_1 = 5 \):

\( \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 2 & -2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0 \)

which gives the eigenvector:

\( v_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} \)

For \( \lambda_2 = 2 \):

\( \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = 0 \)

which gives the eigenvector:

\( v_2 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \)

Applications

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors have numerous applications, including:

  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
  • Stability analysis in systems of differential equations
  • Quantum mechanics
  • Vibrations and dynamics

Conclusion

Understanding eigenvalues and eigenvectors is crucial for many applications in science and engineering. They provide deep insights into the properties of linear transformations and matrices.
